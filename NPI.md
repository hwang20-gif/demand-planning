**NPI** 

**Purpose** 

This process outlines the overall workflow for New Product Introduction (NPI), specifically defining when the Demand Planning department should engage, which discussions they need to participate in, and the system configurations and forecast upload tasks they must complete.

**The core objective is to enable early engagement of Demand Planning in NPI forecast planning. This ensures the team thoroughly understands launch assumptions and is granted sufficient lead time for operations, thereby mitigating planning risks caused by delayed data entry into the system.**

**Definition**

While the overall NPI process involves multiple departments (including product information preparation, SKU/part number creation, costing, and compliance readiness), **the specific workflow relevant to DP is defined as the complete cycle spanning from SKU enablement, pre-release, bottom-up forecast engagement, and forecast upload, all the way through to post-Gate 2 change control.** 

**Importance**

* Resolving Past Pain Points: Previously, DP had to wait until Commercial, Region, BG, Finance, and Leadership had all granted final approval before initiating the forecast upload. This left DP with an extremely narrow operational window, often resulting in "receiving approval today and being expected to integrate it into the planning cycle tomorrow."  
* Early Preparation: The new process integrates DP into the discussions as soon as Commercial / CCM begins establishing the forecast. Instead of waiting idly for final approvals, DP can now complete SKU enablement, data preparation, and assumption alignment ahead of time.  
* Enhancing Forecast Credibility: By providing historical data, look-alike product data, and phase-in/phase-out information, DP can help verify whether the Initial Loading Quantity (ILQ) is sufficient. Furthermore, DP can recommend a realistic ramp-up plan based on historical patterns, preventing the entry of overly aggressive or conservative forecasts at the outset.

**Logic**

### DP loaded by customer or fcst streams, as lower level as it can be per the bottom

### 1\. Forecast Loading Level

* NAM (North America): By customer (e.g., Walmart, Best Buy).  
* Europe: By forecast stream (e.g., Amazon, Central Distribution (CD), Europe Others). *(Note: Ruben needs to update the allocation percentages).*  
* AP / GCN / LATAM: Typically by country.  
* Emerging Markets: Segmentation logic is currently uncertain and requires further confirmation with Mickey or the process owner.

  ### 2\. Timeline & Offset Logic (OSD / DCS / Sell-in Offset)

* OSD (On-Shelf Date) is the actual product launch date in store/online. DCS is typically set around two weeks prior to OSD.  
* The lead time offset for Sell-in and ILQ (Initial Loading Quantity) varies by region and must follow regional lead time logic or the regional NPI Lead Time Matrix (e.g., the matrix used in the AP region).

  ### 3\. ILQ & Ramp-up

* Evaluate historical patterns of look-alike products (e.g., historically, the first month only accounts for 10% of the 12-month total forecast) to determine the ramp-up strategy.  
* Decide whether the new product should ramp up progressively month-by-month, or adopt an aggressive front-loaded forecast in the first three months due to strong market demand.

  ### 4\. Cannibalization & Phase-out

* When a new product acts as a replacement, planners must review the legacy product's sell-out, inventory, and remaining demand.  
* This prevents excessive overlap between new and old forecasts, and avoids over-forecasting the new product while the legacy product still has remaining inventory.

  ### 5\. Statistical Forecast vs. Override

* During the initial NPI phase, Manual Overrides must be used due to a lack of historical data. Statistical forecasts generated by the system—whether based on the "nearest neighbor" method or "13 weeks actuals"—are not entirely reliable at this stage.  
* After approximately 3 to 6 months post-launch, once sufficient actual data is accumulated, evaluate transitioning control to the system by gradually removing the overrides.

  ### 6\. First 3 Months Freeze

* The historical rigid rule requiring the first three months of the forecast to be frozen is no longer enforced. If market conditions shift, adjustments can be made provided they undergo proper alignment and approval.

  ### 7\. Post-Gate 2 Change Management

* Current State: Change requests currently "come from anywhere, such as emails or chat messages," which is strictly unacceptable.  
* Future State: Any pre-launch forecast adjustments—increases or decreases—cannot simply be updated in the system. They must go through formal alignment with GCO and Commercial/CCM, and the Bottom-up template and market assumptions must be updated concurrently.

**SOP**

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch Preparation & System Initialization

### Step 1.1: Prepare NPI Foundational Data

The product team generates new product SKUs, part numbers, costing, and preliminary launch information.

### Step 1.2: Provide Product Roadmap and SKU List

GCO / BG must constantly update the product roadmap for DP starting from this phase. Simultaneously, GCO must share the NPI SKU list ahead of time to initiate subsequent system configurations.

### Step 1.3: Track Timeline and SKU Enablement

DP must utilize the Launch Calendar to track the Gate 2 schedule in advance. The system-side execution for SKU enablement follows this sequence: SKU COSA approval 、Pre-release 、Auto SKU-Org enabled、Manual enablement in the MDM system.

### Step 1.4: System Synchronization and Pre-preparation

Following manual enablement in MDM, a system synchronization period of approximately 24–48 hours is required before data appears in Maestro / NPI Workbook. DP should utilize this waiting window to begin preliminary forecast preparation.

## Phase 2: Bottom-Up Forecast Build-Up & Alignment

### Step 2.1: Initiate Bottom-Up Forecast

Commercial / CCM establishes the baseline forecast for the next 12 months and defines the new product's incrementality, GTM (Go-To-Market) channel strategy (exclusive vs. omni-channel), product categorization, and specific launch objectives (e.g., countering competitors, addressing portfolio gaps, or introducing new technologies).

### Step 2.2: Early DP Engagement and Data Support

DP must engage in the discussions during the initial stages of forecast build-up. DP provides historical Sell-in/Sell-out and phase-out data for legacy products, helps verify the rationality of the Initial Loading Quantity (ILQ), and recommends a realistic ramp-up plan based on historical patterns (e.g., the first month accounting for 10% of the annual total).

### Step 2.3: Finalize Market Assumption Alignment and Documentation

DP and CCM align on specific parameters: monthly quantity breakdown, Average Selling Price (ASP), On-Shelf Date (OSD), seasonality, embargo date, Last Time Buy (LTB), and the cannibalization effect of the phase-in/phase-out transition. This data must be fully documented in the NPI Masterfile and Assumption File. Once completed, a checkbox confirmation must be obtained in the Launch Calendar.

## Phase 3: Approval Gate

### Step 3.1: Regional Review

Regions review and approve their respective forecasts.

### Step 3.2: Final Leadership Approval

GCO / Finance / Leadership make the final decision.

\[Mandatory Rule\]: DP must receive an official approval email that includes the GCO LT, Finance representative Mohammed, and Global Head of Retail Frederic Derreumaux before being authorized to upload.

### Step 3.3: Secure Operational Lead Time

Upon receiving the complete approval email, DP must ensure a minimum of 1 week of lead time for forecast load & validation to prepare for the system upload.

## Phase 4: System Execution

### Step 4.1: Timeline Parameter Calculation (Offset Setting)

DP calculates the DCS timing based on the OSD (typically 2 weeks prior). Based on regional lead time logic or the regional NPI Lead Time Matrix, DP calculates and sets the specific timing for ILQ and Sell-in.

### Step 4.2: Execute Lowest-Grain Forecast Load (NPI Workbook Upload)

DP loads the forecast into the NPI Workbook within the Maestro system. The loading logic dictates that ILQ is placed in Month 1, DCS is distributed by month, and the remainder is driven by conversion rates. The forecast must be strictly inputted at the Lowest Level per region:

* NAM (North America): By customer (e.g., Walmart, Best Buy).  
* Europe: By forecast stream (e.g., Amazon, CD). For "Europe Others," the owner, Ruben, must calculate and update the allocation percentages.  
* AP / GCN / LATAM: By country.  
* Emerging Markets: No unified standard; classification must be confirmed with the owner, Micky, or the regional head prior to uploading.

### Step 4.3: Post-Upload Review

DP performs system checks to identify any missing data, wrong levels, timing issues, or system synchronization delays.

## Phase 5: Post-Launch Control

### Step 5.1: Post-Gate 2 Change Management

If market conditions shift before the product launch (post-Gate 2), Commercial / CCM initiates a change request.

\[Strict Prohibition\]: Requesting system changes casually via email or chat messages is strictly prohibited. All changes must undergo formal alignment between GCO and Commercial.

### Step 5.2: Update Bottom-Up Templates

Once changes are aligned, CCM is responsible for updating the Bottom-up Forecast Template and its underlying market assumptions to the latest version. After the update is complete, the template is handed over to DP to execute the system data adjustments.

### Step 5.3: Post-Launch Monitoring & Manual Override Phase-Out

DP continuously monitors actual demand, DCS, Sell-in, and Sell-out pre- and post-launch. Because statistical forecasts (Stat Forecast) are unreliable in the early stages, manual overrides are utilized initially. After 3–6 months post-launch, if actual data is sufficient, DP should evaluate phasing out overrides to transition routine planning to standard system logic.

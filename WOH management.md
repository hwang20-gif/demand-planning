### 🎯 Purpose
To establish a standardized, cross-functional collaboration mechanism for defining, aligning, and executing Week on Hand (WOH) Targets across markets. This ensures full synchronization among Demand Planning (DP), Finance, and Customer Supply Chain (CSC), grounding Finance's Gross Revenue (GR) Plan and CSC's Order Fulfillment in a unified inventory target to maximize operational and financial efficiency. 

---

### 📘 Definition
* **WOH (Week on Hand):** Inventory target measured in weeks, indicating how many weeks of future demand the current inventory level can sustain.  
* **Supercluster:** The highest level of regional segmentation within Logitech (e.g., APM, GCN, NAM, LAT, EURO).  
* **Customer Group / Subgroup:** Customer-based segmentation (e.g., specific key account categories, or specific countries/markets like Taiwan, Hong Kong, Japan).  
* **Product Line (PL) / Product Group (PG):** Product-based segmentation (e.g., Gaming, PWS, Canova).  
* **ABC Classification:** A framework that categorizes products into A, B, or C levels based on their strategic importance, typically driven by sales volume or value contribution.

---

### 💡 Importance
* **Alignment of Financial and Operational Metrics:** WOH targets directly influence Finance’s financial/inventory targets and the GR Plan, as well as CSC’s replenishment and order execution. Failing to align upfront results in a disconnect between financial budgeting and actual supply chain operations.
* **Market-Tailored Flexibility:** Because planning logic varies across global markets (e.g., North America focuses heavily on major retailers, while Asia-Pacific focuses on country-specific dynamics and strategic products), the WOH framework must support multi-dimensional configurations to prevent one-size-fits-all stockouts or inventory piles.

---

### ⚙️ WOH Management Logic
* **Multi-Dimensional Flexibility:** WOH targets should be defined at the most appropriate granularity depending on business needs (ranging broadly from the Supercluster level down to a specific Customer like Best Buy or an individual SKU), rather than sequentially passing through every single level.  
* **Regional Market Practices:**
  * **NAM:** Primarily driven by Customer Group level.  
  * **APM / GCN:** Does not strictly enforce ABC classification; certain "C items" may require a higher WOH target due to specific country or customer requirements to avoid stockouts.  
  * **Emerging Markets:** Retains ABC Classification as part of the core logic.  
  * **EURO:** Tends to be managed at the Category / Product Line level.

---

### 📋 Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

#### Step 1: Initiate WOH Management
* **Owner:** Demand Planning (DP)  
* **Action:** Launch the annual Week on Hand (WOH) management process to serve as the baseline for supply-demand balancing and financial planning.

#### Step 2: Determine WOH Target Granularity
* **Owner:** Demand Planning (DP)  
* **Action:** Assess regional market characteristics and planning practices to select the optimal granularity for inventory targets.  
* **Available Dimensions (Flexible Options):**  
  * *Supercluster:* Highest regional level (e.g., APM, GCN, NAM, etc.).  
  * *Customer Group / Subgroup:* Key account categories or specific country/market levels.  
  * *PG Level:* Product Group level.  
  * *ABC Classification:* Strategic product importance segmentation (updated quarterly).

#### Step 3: Departmental Review and Agreement (WOH Target Agreement)
* **Owners:** Finance / CSC  
* **Action:**  
  * *Finance:* Review the proposed WOH targets against financial and inventory goals to secure the WOH Target Agreement.  
  * *CSC:* Evaluate the targets against supply chain capacity and allocation feasibility to secure the WOH Target Agreement.

#### Step 4: Conduct Annual WOH Target Alignment
* **Owner:** Demand Planning (DP) (Lead) / Finance & CSC (Participants)  
* **Action:** Consolidate the defined planning dimensions, financial requirements, and supply chain constraints into a final three-way alignment.  
* **Cadence:** Executed once a year in **January** to officially lock the baseline targets for the next fiscal year.

#### Step 5: Drive Financial & Revenue Plans
* **Owners:** Finance / Demand Planning (DP)  
* **Action:** Apply the aligned WOH targets into financial planning systems to generate:  
  * *Finance:* GR Target  
  * *DP:* GR Plan  

#### Step 6: Execute Order & Shipment Fulfillment
* **Owner:** CSC  
* **Action:** Deploy the aligned WOH targets as the single source of truth for inventory control to drive Order/Shipment Fulfillment for direct customers, ensuring optimal supply availability without inventory liability.

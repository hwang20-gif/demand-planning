### 🎯 Purpose & Global Consensus
* **Standardize Order Locking:** Define a unified framework for the demand plan frozen window to stabilize factory loading and streamline supply chains.
* **Global Implementation:** Circulate and align the frozen window guidelines with all regional planning teams to ensure cross-functional execution.

---

### 🌐 Regional Frozen Window Guidelines

#### 📦 NAM (North America)
* **Frozen Period:** Suggested **6-week** frozen period.
* **Action Item:** Requires further discussion on *forecast vs. expedites* under this scenario. A comprehensive revamp of the expedite process and ladder alignment is mandatory.

#### 🌎 LAT (Latin America)
* **Frozen Period:** Strict **13-week** frozen window specifically for **B2B deals**.
* **Exception Rule:** Regional planners will verify supply availability with GPP (Global Production Planning). If approved, an expedite window of **2 to 3 weeks** will be applied.

#### 🇪🇺 Europe
* **Frozen Period:** Strict **8-week** frozen window.
* **Flexibility Rule:** Limited flexibility is allowed for weeks **9 to 13**.

#### 🌏 EM (Emerging Markets)
* **Frozen Period:** Strict **8-week** frozen window.
* **Flexibility Rule:** Limited flexibility is allowed for weeks **9 to 13**.

#### 🇨🇳 GCN (Greater China)
* **Frozen Period:** Formula-based window derived from Lead Time (L/T).
* **Calculation:** `4 weeks Production L/T + In-transit L/T` tailored for different destination countries.

#### 🌏 APM (Asia Pacific Markets)
* **Frozen Period:** Formula-based window derived from Lead Time (L/T).
* **Calculation:** `4 weeks Production L/T + In-transit L/T` tailored for different destination countries.
